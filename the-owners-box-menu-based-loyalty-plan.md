# 2. What Rewards Should NOT Be

Do **not** make rewards too open-ended, such as:

- “Use points for anything”
- “Free steak at a low point threshold”
- “Free seafood whenever”
- “Convert points straight to cash with no controls”

That is how loyalty programs become an expensive apology letter.

The higher-cost items on this menu are likely:
- crab dip
- crab cake
- salmon
- rib eye
- NY strip
- picanha
- loaded short rib nachos
- shrimp items
- steak & eggs

These should be:
- premium rewards
- milestone unlocks
- promo-limited
- attached to spend thresholds

Not casual low-tier freebies.

# 4. Core Point Earning Model

## Base earn rate
**1 point per $1 spent on direct app orders**

This applies to:
- in-store pickup
- curbside pickup
- future direct web/app checkout

Not applied to:
- tax
- tips
- gift card purchases
- third-party delivery marketplace orders

## Why this rate
It is easy to understand and easy to market.

---

# 5. Internal Reward Math

## Recommended target effective value
Aim for an average effective customer return of:

- **5% to 8% back** on normal behavior
- **8% to 12% back** during bonus periods or promos

That is enough to feel valuable without setting the restaurant on fire financially.

## Point value model
Use this internal logic:

- **100 points ≈ $5 in reward value**
- so **1 point ≈ $0.05 internal redemption value**

This does **not** mean every reward must follow exact retail value.
It means reward thresholds should be roughly balanced around that value.

Example:
- 50 points should feel like a meaningful small reward
- 100 points should feel like a real win
- 250+ points should feel premium

# 6. Menu-Based Reward Ladder

These are the recommended reward tiers based on the actual menu structure.

## Tier 1 — Easy Wins
These get people hooked early.

### 25 points
- Free fountain drink / soft drink
- or free side sauce / dip add-on

### 40 points
- Free fries or side upgrade
- or free brunch coffee / tea if applicable operationally

### 50 points
- Free dessert-style add-on or simple side perk
- good candidate: chips, side salad upgrade, or brunch side bonus

## Tier 2 — Small Food Reward
These feel real and keep people engaged.

### 75 points
- Free Queso & Chorizo add-on size or discounted appetizer credit
- or free cauliflower wings small portion if ops support it
- or free breakfast side bundle during brunch

### 85 points
- Free Classic Caesar
- or free Chopped House Salad
- or free fries upgrade + drink combo

## Tier 3 — Strong Mid-Tier Reward
This is where people start feeling “this app is worth using.”

### 100 points
- $5 off direct order
- safest universal mid-tier reward

### 125 points
Choose one:
- Free Queso & Chorizo
- Free Crispy Calamari
- Free Classic Sandwich upgrade credit
- Free breakfast item from selected list

This is a strong sweet spot.

## Tier 4 — Appetizer / Sandwich Tier
### 150 points
Choose one:
- Free The Bar Chicken sandwich
- Free Chicago sandwich
- Free Breakfast Sandwich
- Free Buttermilk Pancakes
- Free French Toast

This tier works because these items have strong perceived value without being the most dangerous cost-wise.

## Tier 5 — Premium Casual Tier
### 200 points
Choose one:
- $10 off $25+
- Free jumbo wings (6 count)
- Free burger/sandwich class item from approved list
- Free brunch entree from approved list

This is where you begin driving larger basket behavior.

## Tier 6 — Party / Shareable Tier
### 250 points
Choose one:
- Free pizza from approved list
- Free wings (12 count) with purchase threshold
- Free appetizer sampler-style promo
- $15 off $40+

This tier should be guarded by approved menu list or minimum spend.

## Tier 7 — High Value / Promo Tier
### 300 points
Choose one:
- Free Classic Neapolitan or Cheese Pizza
- Free Supreme or Meat Lover with spend threshold
- Game Day Combo credit
- Free Loaded Nachos with order minimum

This should feel big.

## Tier 8 — Rare / Event Tier
### 500 points
Use for:
- game-night bundle
- special merch
- reserved premium brunch offer
- premium combo package
- watch party reward

Do **not** make this a default free steak tier.

# 9. Sports-Bar-Specific Multipliers

This is where The Owner’s Box gets more interesting than a generic pizza loyalty app.

## Weekly bonus examples
- **2x points on NFL Sundays**
- **2x points during college football Saturdays**
- **2x points during UFC fight nights**
- **2x points for playoff games shown in-app**
- **Brunch bonus Sundays from 9 AM – 2 PM**

## Suggested bonus structure
### Simple rule
- 1 point per $1 normally
- 2 points per $1 during selected event windows
- 3 points per $1 only for special promo nights

Do not run 3x constantly unless you enjoy giving away margin in broad daylight.

# 10. Game-Day Missions

These are better than random coupons.

## Good mission examples
- Order wings + pizza = **+15 bonus points**
- First curbside order = **+20 points**
- Place 3 direct orders in one month = **+30 points**
- Brunch order on two consecutive Sundays = **+25 points**
- Order during featured game window = **+10 points**
- Try a featured special = **+10 points**

These push behavior instead of just discounting.

# 11. Reward Logic That Favors Everybody

The goal is not just “reward whales.”

The system should favor:

## Casual customers
They need quick early wins.
That is why:
- 25 pts
- 50 pts
- 75 pts
matter.

A guest spending $20–30 a visit should feel progress quickly.

## Regular customers
They should feel like the app is worth returning to.
That is why:
- 100 pts
- 125 pts
- 150 pts
must unlock real rewards people actually want.

## Big spend / group orders
These customers should have meaningful milestone rewards.
That is why:
- 200+
- 250+
- 300+
should map to pizza, wings, game-day bundle, or meaningful discounts.
